What's in the vault?

▦

Live index dashboard

A searchable, filterable index of every song in your archive — always up to date, no upkeep.

⟳

Semi-automated metadata

Fill in the title, album, and artist; the vault gathers the rest. Release year, genre, label, duration, cover art, and streaming links from MusicBrainz and the Cover Art Archive.

✎

Artist pages that build themselves

Every artist in your archive automatically gets a page with song, album, and favorite counts and a live table of their songs. Customize them as you'd like.

♪

Key detection In progress

A "detect key from chords" link in the song header analyzes your chord sheet and writes the key to frontmatter — including the sounding key when a capo is set.

☰

Plain Markdown all the way down

Your archive is a folder of text files. No database, no lock-in. Version it however you like. Add whatever you want (videos, links, notes, etc.)

Up and running in three steps

  1. Get the vault

    Download the latest release, unzip it wherever you keep your notes. Prefer GitHub? Use this template on GitHub and clone yours, or clone the repo directly.

  2. Open it in Obsidian

    Open folder as vault, enable community plugins, and install the four the vault is pre-configured for: Templater, Datacore, Chord Sheets, and Vextab.

  3. Add a song

    Create a note in Songs/. Use the song title to name the file, fill in Artist and Album metadata, add your tab or chords to the body, and run Enrich Song to gather the rest of the metadata. The index and artist page take care of themselves.

The repo ships no song content — your own transcriptions of copyrighted songs belong in your private vault, not a public fork. Fetched cover art stays © its rights holders, with attribution stored on every image.
